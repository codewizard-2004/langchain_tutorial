## 1. Introduction

Pizza is a globally celebrated flatbread-based dish originating from Italy, renowned for its simplicity, adaptability, and cultural significance. At its core, pizza consists of a fermented wheat-based dough topped with tomato sauce, cheese, and various ingredients, then baked at high temperatures. Despite its humble beginnings, pizza has evolved into a sophisticated culinary domain encompassing science, tradition, and regional identity.

---

## 2. Historical Background

Pizza traces its roots to Naples, Italy, where it emerged as a staple food for the working class in the late 18th and early 19th centuries. Early forms were simple flatbreads topped with oil, herbs, and later tomatoes after their introduction to Europe.

A defining milestone was the creation of Pizza Margherita, representing the Italian flag through tomatoes (red), mozzarella (white), and basil (green). Through migration and globalization, pizza spread worldwide and adapted to regional tastes.

---

## 3. Core Components of Pizza

### 3.1 Dough

The dough is the structural foundation of pizza and largely determines texture, flavor, and digestibility.

**Basic Ingredients:**
- Wheat flour (Tipo 00 or bread flour)
- Water
- Yeast
- Salt
- Olive oil (optional)

**Key Characteristics:**
- Gluten development for elasticity
- Fermentation for flavor complexity
- Hydration level affecting softness and air pockets

---

### 3.2 Sauce

Pizza sauce is traditionally made from crushed tomatoes, often uncooked to retain freshness.

**Common Additions:**
- Salt
- Olive oil
- Garlic
- Basil or oregano

The sauce should enhance the dough rather than dominate it.

---

### 3.3 Cheese

Cheese contributes richness, meltability, and browning.

**Common Choices:**
- Mozzarella (fresh or low-moisture)
- Provolone
- Parmesan (used sparingly as a finish)

Cheese quality directly impacts texture and flavor balance.

---

### 3.4 Toppings

Toppings define the identity and style of a pizza.

**Categories:**
- Vegetables: mushrooms, onions, peppers, olives
- Proteins: pepperoni, sausage, chicken, anchovies
- Herbs and extras: basil, chili flakes, olive oil

Overloading toppings is discouraged, as it affects baking and structural integrity.

## 5. Major Pizza Styles

### 5.1 Neapolitan
- Soft, airy crust
- Minimal toppings
- High-temperature baking

### 5.2 New York Style
- Thin, foldable slices
- Crisp yet chewy crust
- Large diameter pizzas

### 5.3 Chicago Deep Dish
- Thick crust
- Layered fillings
- Sauce on top

### 5.4 Sicilian
- Rectangular shape
- Thick, fluffy base
